A first workshop with stakeholder was done by Service Designer colleagues to better understand the need and collect the requirements.

Based on the principal requirements found during the workshop, different use cases were defined. Then I created a task flow for each use case, starting to describe first interface sections and options.

Once done, together with a Service Designer and a developer, we made 3 Service blueprints to group the findings, define the user journey and analyse every touchpoint. This way it was easier to collect the respective needs and insights and better establish the interface requirements, essential part to start the wireframe phase.

Research

Designed at DOING - Part of Capgemini

A project for a Tobacco company brand with the aim to introduce inside the selling process a product configurator and implement it in three different touchpoints:

the e-commerce, the offline stores and the remote selling channel.

The product configurator allows to explore the models of the tobacco heater, personalise them in colors and accessories.

Overview

Once gathered all the useful information, it was the wireframing phase turn. It was guided with a mobile first mindset and helped to define the navigation, the entire flow of interaction and the final structure of each page.

I started with a raw wireframe to gather the first feedback from stakeholder and validate a design direction. I was proposing two variants of product navigation and this starting feedback was essential to decide which path to follow.


Then I moved to an hi-fy wireframe approach and created a browsable prototype on Figma to better convey the entire flow of interaction, validate it and design the responsive versions for tablet, desktop and totem.

Design

The project was launched as MVP on Christmas 2020 in few stores and in the remote selling channel. On a survey took after 2 months from launch:

- 90% of consumers in store said they found the configurator useful to explore the product while waiting their turn;

- 74% said they discovered new accessories they didin’t know about;

- 72% said it was easier with this tool personalize the product.


After its launch on the ecommerce on March 2021, the product configurator have improved the conversion rate of 4.17% (all purchase/sessions).
