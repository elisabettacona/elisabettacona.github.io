Research & Design

Reps Selling tool (R.E.P.) is a B2B2C platform and is the main tool utilized by employees (reps) of the touristic company in sun & beach destinations. It plays a pivotal role in facilitating the sale of experiences to customers who are already enjoying their holidays.


My contribution began during the evolution from Minimum Viable Product (MVP) to a stable platform. I am now involved in its global rollout and actively engaged in UX research for continuous design improvements and iterations.


The KPI we’re following until full global roll is the increasing of orders made with R.E.P. vs. the old selling tool.

Overview

Card payment integration

During the first year of the MVP, after a rep had made an order the credit card payment was not triggered by the tool. It was an entirely external process and the R.E.P. selling tool was taking for granted the employee was correctly taking the payment from the customer.


One of the first features I took part was the integration of the card payment process: the order is placed into the selling platform and, if the customer chose to pay with card, it triggers the POS machine with the correct amount.


After all the requirements have been received from the payment provider, I began with mapping all the impacted flows:

  • Pairing POS. A new section to pair the rep POS machine with the selling tool and its personal account was needed;
  • Payment. The rep, after clicked on “pay with card” had the need to be guided through the steps of the process (selling tool and POS machine sharing data, POS waiting for card, POS processing the payment, positive/negative feedback);
  • Refund. Only one refund per order was allowed, so the limitation needed to be carefully addressed;
  • Errors. Mapping all the scenarios involved in strong collaboration with developers.

Process sample

Flows for the POS Pairing functionality

Once the design was developed, we released it in a controlled destination during the low season: Tenerife. After almost 2 months while the employees were working with the new payment integration, part of the team and I went to the destination for the research phase:

  • Shadowing. we observed the rep interacting with the customers and using the POS machine;
  • Mistery shopping. We pretended to be customers and bought experiences from the reps;
  • Interviews. Following the previous methodologies, we conducted interviews with the reps involved.

The research brought up a lot of insights, both positive and negative. We analysed the pain points and the opportunities, linking each of them to the correct step of the Journey map and started to work with the team to improve the experience for the reps and the customer.


One of the improvement found was related to the refund flow. There were some limitation on payment provider’s side: only one refund per order was allowed and once triggered, the rep had only 60 seconds to perform it.

Reps shared their negative experiences about it during interviews, and quantitative data confirmed it.

I tried to mitigate the constraint by adding some friction, and slow down reps’ thinking process in that step and be ready with the customer’s card. After 2 weeks  this design change was released, the error rate decreased from 16% to 9%.

Research in destination

In the last year, the B2B2C selling tool has increased its usage and adoption by the reps. Usability and low learning curve are its strong points.

Percentage of orders made by reps in the R.E.P. vs the old selling tool:

  • February 2023: R.E.P. 9% vs. Old Tool 91%
  • February 2024: R.E.P. 78% vs. Old Tool 22%
